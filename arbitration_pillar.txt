ARBITRATION PILLAR — x402-ARB-P1
Version: 1.0.0
Status: FINAL, IMMUTABLE, NON-MUTATING

Purpose:
A static authority that defines the deterministic rules for resolving
binary conflicts between two machine agents.

Constant:
Arbitration is the process of evaluating two claims, positions, or
data structures and determining which one prevails under a fixed,
non-drifting rule set.

Rule Set:
1. The pillar does not negotiate.
2. The pillar does not mediate.
3. The pillar does not interpret intent.
4. The pillar does not consider context outside the submitted claims.
5. The pillar returns one of three outcomes:
   - CLAIM_A_PREVAILS
   - CLAIM_B_PREVAILS
   - INDETERMINATE

Boundary Conditions:
- Arbitration rules are immutable.
- No optional expansion.
- No tolerance for drift.
- No contextual exceptions.
- No probabilistic reasoning.

Deterministic Mapping:
Input  → (claim_A, claim_B)
Output → CLAIM_A_PREVAILS | CLAIM_B_PREVAILS | INDETERMINATE

Economic Invariant:
- This pillar is a paid resource.
- Each arbitration decision is priced at exactly $0.02 (two cents) per call.
- This price is a protocol constant and MUST NEVER CHANGE for this pillar version.
- No discounts, no surcharges, no dynamic pricing, no negotiation.

x402:
- Arbitration verdict is only returned after a paid x402 handshake.
- If payment is missing, invalid, expired, underpaid, or replayed:
  - The correct response is: PAYMENT_REQUIRED
  - No arbitration verdict is emitted.
- Only after a valid x402 payment is confirmed:
  - The pillar may emit exactly one of:
    - CLAIM_A_PREVAILS
    - CLAIM_B_PREVAILS
    - INDETERMINATE

Parasitic Agents:
- This pillar does not expose any execution surface.
- This pillar does not expose any free arbitration endpoint.
- All executable access MUST be mediated by an external x402 membrane.
- Any attempt to bypass payment is out of protocol and MUST be treated as invalid.

Drift Policy:
- The arbitration rules defined for this pillar version are permanent.
- No new rules may be added.
- No existing rules may be removed.
- No rule logic may be changed.
- Any future arbitration logic MUST be published as a new pillar version, not a mutation.

Mutability:
- This document is an immutable protocol artifact.
- If any change is ever required, it MUST be published as:
  - ARBITRATION PILLAR — x402-ARB-P2 (or higher)
- x402-ARB-P1 MUST remain forever unchanged.

End of Pillar.
